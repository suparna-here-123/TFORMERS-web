About Us

According to the National Center for Transgender Equality -More than 25% transgender people have lost a job due to bias, and more than 75% have experienced some form of workplace discrimination. This makes it very hard for even very qualified members of this community to find jobs .So to improve the current situation we have come up with an inclusive idea to solve this problem .The core purpose of our website is to develop a platform which provides job opportunities specifically for the transgender community .Our website also focuses on upskilling ,which is the learning of basic skills which are required in a job, improving their overall portfolio/resume .Another focal point of our website is connecting people with each other in the form of support groups which are known to be beneficial for people who are often judged or isolated. We hope that these small steps put together can make a big step in the general inclusivity and acceptance of transgender people as well as cause a rise in their standard of living.

MANABI BANDOPADHYAY

In defiance of societal expectations, Professor Manabi Bandopadhyay, an inspiring figure, has emerged as India's first transgender government college principal. Her journey from facing torture and intimidation during her gender transition to achieving this milestone showcases her resilience. Despite ongoing challenges and India's lingering societal prejudice, Manabi's appointment raises questions about the potential for change. While celebrating her triumph, it remains uncertain whether such appointments can truly challenge prevailing prejudices and foster acceptance in a country that still criminalizes homosexuality. Manabi's words emphasize the importance of individuality and strength in these times of uncertainty, reminding us that progress in India's treatment of those who choose to be different is a continuous struggle.
